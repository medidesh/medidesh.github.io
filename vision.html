1 PROBLEM

The pharmacy sector is one of the most critical parts of healthcare, yet it remains digitally underserved.

Key issues observed across emerging markets:

  • Manual or semi-digital inventory management
  • Poor expiry and batch tracking
  • High risk of expired medicine circulation
  • Slow billing due to counter-based POS dependency
  • No real-time visibility across staff or branches
  • Generic retail POS systems that ignore pharmacy-specific workflows

Despite being a regulated, high-volume industry, most pharmacies operate without proper software infrastructure.

2 MARKET OPPORTUNITY

Global

  • Over 4 million pharmacies worldwide
  • Majority are small to mid-sized independent pharmacies

Bangladesh

  • Estimated 150,000+ retail pharmacies
  • Extremely low penetration of modern pharmacy software
  • High daily transaction volume
  • Growing pressure for compliance and efficiency

This represents a large, fragmented, and under-digitized market with strong long-term demand.

3 EXISTING SOLUTIONS FAIL

1) Generic POS Systems

  • Retail-first, not pharmacy-first
  • No batch or expiry intelligence
  • Counter-limited operations
  • Minimal compliance support

2) Enterprise Pharmacy Systems

  • Expensive
  • Complex to operate
  • Poor adoption by small pharmacies
  • Hardware and training heavy

There is a clear gap between affordability, usability, and pharmacy-specific needs.

4 SOLUTION — MEDIDESH

Medidesh is a cloud-based pharmacy operating system built specifically for pharmacies in emerging markets.

It replaces traditional counter-based POS with a flexible, multi-device workflow that allows multiple staff to operate simultaneously from mobile or desktop.
